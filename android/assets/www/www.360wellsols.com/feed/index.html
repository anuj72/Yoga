]]> Rishikesh is a city in Dehradun district which lies in one of the Indian state, Uttrakhand situated in the foothills of the Himalayas. It is also known as “Yoga Capital of the World”. It is approximately 25 km north as one of the sacred places in India, Haridwar and 43 km southeast from state capital Dehradun. Population of Rishikesh is 102,138 which makes it seventh most populated city in Uttrakhand state. It is very popular among the pilgrims and spiritual seekers and is considered as one of the holy places of Hindus. Hindu saints have been visiting Rishikesh since ancient times to gain knowledge and practice meditation. Non-vegetarian food and alcohol is strictly banned in Rishikesh because of its spiritual importance.

How to reach Rishikesh

Rishikesh is well connected by road, rail, and air transport system.
By Road – It is approximately 225 km from Delhi airport. Local as well as foreign tourists can reach Rishikesh either by taxi or by Bus service. It takes approximately 6-7 hours to reach there from Delhi. There is frequent, Haridwar – Rishikesh bus service, which takes approximately 25-30 min as well as Rishikesh-Dehradun bus service, which takes approximately 45min – 1 hour. Tourists also have option of taxi from these places also.
By Rail – Rishikesh has well connected railway network. Wellness seekers can easily reach Rishikesh from major cities through trains.
Nearest Airport – Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun) is the nearest airport. It is 15 km away from Rishikesh.

Must Experience of Ayurveda, Meditation and Yoga at Rishikesh

Rishikesh has Ayurveda, Meditation and Yoga centers. Seekers from all over the world come here for various types of Ayurveda treatment, Ayurvedic massages, and learning and practicing yoga which makes them feel relaxed and also help them to get rid of many health issues.
Meditation enhances concentration and also provides tension free environment. Here in Rishikesh, wellness seekers can meditate near the holy river Ganga and can spend time in ashrams learning different things. Meditating near river Ganga is itself a unique experience, negative ions of river makes you feel rejuvenated and sound of river helps you in meditation. Apart from this, Ganga Aarti is one of the main attractions, held at the ghats of Ganga in Parmarth ashram.
There are many world level yoga centers, where wellness seekers can learn yoga under the guidance of well-trained yoga instructers.
Camping – Rishikesh is situated near holy river Ganga, wellness seekers can experience Camping on riverside. Echoes of river rapids and amazing natural surroundings attract wellness seekers from all part of the world. Nearby Rishikesh there is Rajaji National Park, wellness seekers can experience camping in jungle between the hills, under the open sky.
Besides camping, wellness seekers can experience other activities at or from their camping sites:

  • Bonfire in night
  • River Rafting
  • Rock climbing
  • Bungee jumping

When to visit Rishikesh

Best time to visit Rishikesh is September- April.
Note: In the month of July, there is a festival called Savan in Rishikesh in which thousands of people take part, due to which major roads are blocked and all the hotels near by are booked. So please avoid travelling to Rishikesh during this period of time.

]]> Naturally recurring altered consciousness of mind and body is known as sleeping. Dozing is composed of a series cycles of 90-minute during which brain moves from deep, non-rapid eye movement (non-REM) sleep to REM sleep. This cycle is generally stable throughout the night. However, ratio of non-REM to REM sleep changes. Head of the Sleep and Neuroimaging Lab at the University of California, Berkeley, Dr. Matt Walker explains that “non-REM sleep tends to dominate slumber cycles in the early part of the night. But as clock creeps toward daybreak, REM sleep muscles in.” That can be considered significant, because as per some researches non-REM sleep is deeper and more restorative than lighter, dream-infused REM sleep, however, both offer important benefits.

We have window of several hours between 8 pm till 12 am, during this our brain has the chance to get all REM and non-REM cycles.

Perfect bedtime:
Dr. Matt Walker further explains that the regardless of when one goes to bed, shift from non-REM to REM sleep happens at certain times of the night. Therefore if someone hit the bed very late, for example 3 AM-sleep will certainly tilt toward REM-heavy sleep. Reduction in deep, restorative sleep may leave you groggy, tired, and blunt-minded the next day. Negative thoughts easily enter a tired and groggy mind.

One study shows, people had lower scores on standardized tests of memory and processing speed had an experience working at night. People who had a decade or more of shift work or going to sleep late in the night experience had such pronounced cognitive deficits that were equivalent to about 6.5 years of cognitive decline.

It’s not a good idea that to learn to work at night and sleep during the day. Body’s circadian rhythms that regulate everything from sleeping patterns to energy levels through thyroid hormone, digestion, movement of bowels, and hunger levels tells brain the time to go to sleep as per day pattern. It’s considered to be not natural to reset or reschedule circadian rhythms when it comes to bedtime.

Duration of sleep:
As per National Sleep Foundation recommendation adults should get the following amount of sleep:

  • Adults — 7.5 to 9 hours daily.
  • Older Adults 65+ — 7 to 8 hours daily.

Shortened sleep has many effects, people who slept for five hours a night for just a week
had a higher heart rate during the day as per one of the recent study.

As per study from Paul Kelley and Oxford University, ideal wakeup time when we are in our
20s is 9:30 a.m.; in our 30s is 8 a.m.; in our 40s is 7:30 a.m.; in our 50s is 7 a.m.; and in 60s 6:30 a.m.

As per one of the traditional medicines, from 11pm to 3am most of your blood circulation
concentrates in your liver that helps in detoxification process. Liver then neutralizes and breaks
down body toxins accumulated throughout the day. Therefore sleeping at this for the smooth
process to be carried becomes very important. It is believed that:

  • Going to sleep at 11pm, have full 4 hours to detoxify your body.
  • Going to sleep at 12am, you have 3 hours.
  • Going to sleep at 1am, you have 2 hours.
  • And going to sleep at 2am, you only have 1 hour to detoxify.

It has been researched that sleeping late and/or less than 7.5 hours of sleep daily can lead to chronic
health problems like high blood pressure, heart disease, impaired brain activity, depression, memory
problems, cognitive dysfunction, weight gain, impact on child bearing, hallucinations, thyroid
disorder, and type 2 diabetes.

Dr. Matt Walker says that it is very paramount for everyone specifically students, child
bearing females, and 60+ aged adults to understand the ideal sleep time and duration for living a
healthy and prosperous life, he further adds that people suffering from different medical condition
ranging from fever, pregnancy, endocrinal disorder specially the thyroid disorder, and cardio
disorder etc. to heed to the advice and take care of their sleeping time and duration to avoid any
further deterioration and recover quickly.

Ayurvedic therapy system considers human to be made up of five elements and considers to be miniature of universe, that is to say that the vastness of universe lies within humans.  Five elements are Earth, Fire, Water, Air, and Ether.  Now these five elements combine in pairs to form three doshas or life forces.  The dynamism of this makes life happen.  Ether and Air makes up Vata.  Fire and water makes up Pitta.  Water and earth makes up Kapha.  These three forces keep on changing dynamically to make life happen.  These forces are influenced by the thoughts, environment, diet, lifestyle, and time of the day.  When all these factors are in sync with nature and in line with betterment of living being produces beautiful impacts.  The Ayurvedic therapies have been designed to balance out these forces, however, before applying any of the therapy a complete consultation is required since every body type is unique and a Doctor or Vedacharya can only decide the appropriate therapy.

To conclude, we can say that Ayurveda being one of the finest medical sciences teaches us that everything is within us, and by taking a positive outlook and ignoring the negative happenings, we should be able to get the best results out of us.

]]> Dharamshala city in Himachal Pradesh, India is in the upper heights of the Kangra valley with average altitude of 1,475 meters above sea level. The area consists of highly dense deodar cedar trees. Upper Dharamshala is known as McLeodganj and is also known as “Little Lhasa”with major strong hold of Tibetan refugees and is also the seat of the 14thDalai Lama of the Tibetan sect of Buddhism. People also call it as “Mcllo”.

McLeodganj has been named after a British governor of the province “Sir Ian McLeod”. It is popular tourist destination for both the Indians and foreigners due to the presence of Tibetan settlement, Ayurveda and Yoga centers, and its picturesque surroundings. Multi-coloured prayer flags can be seen festooning its streets. McLeodganj and Dharamshala has been welcoming seekers of spiritual bliss; providing a respite before the snow clad Dhauladhar range.

Nearest airport is Gaggal airport that is about 22 kms from McLeodganj. By road McLeodganj is 507 km NW of Delhi, it takes close to 11 hours to drive and reach here from Delhi.

The lifelines of McLeodGanj are its Temples; and two main streets that make up the beautiful market for tourists. Dalai Lama’s Temple or TsuglagKhang is one of the major tourist attraction where you can see people trying to meditate along with monks. Calmness permeate through winds and soaks you completely altogether.

Market is famous for its restaurants and cafes selling Tibetan,German, Italian, Japanese, Israeli, American, and endless flavours of Indianfood. Trek and Dine is one of the famous cafe situated near trekking track in Dharamkot. Tourist also find interesting the traditionally hand knit woollens, prayer flags, prayer bells, thangkas, masks, and incense sticks. Trekkers can find trekking gear at competitive prices. From main market, roads go towards Naddi, Dharamkot, Bhagsu Nag, and lower Dharamshala. Naddi has got picturesque views. There is a famous Sahajayoga Meditation Centre in Naddi that teaches meditation for free. Another meditation center known as Tushita is in Dharamkot and teaches Buddhist Meditation. There are few Ayurvedic, wellness, and Yoga centers in Naddi, Dharamkot, and Bhagsu Nag that takes care of the wellness seekers reaching here from different countries.

Norbulingka(Jewel Park) is Tibetan institute of art and culture in Sidhbari that is about 14 kms from McLeodganj. It’s an institute that preserves and promotes rich Tibetan culture. Thangkas are made that serves a visual aid to prayer and meditation. It has a Losel Doll Museum with about 160 crafted dolls displaying regional, ritual, religious, official, and theatrical costumes of Tibet.

Triund and Indradhar pass are one of the famous easy to moderate treks, and depending on route selection it can take 5 days of time to complete the entire route starting from McLeodganj to Triund, Triund to Lahesh cave, Lahesh cave to ChhataParao via Indradhar pass, Chhataparao to Kwarsi, and Kwarsi-Hilling and Choli.

April to June and August to November are the best timings to visit McLeodganj. July being the rainy season can be rejuvenating to see rain falling through dense Deodar Pine trees. One should visit McLeodganj and surrounding areas with complete schedule to enjoy each moment of their stay.
